<div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p>CSSS Meeting Minutes<br>
August 20, 2008, 6PM</p>
<p>In attendance: Giuliana Villegas, Michele Ng, David Lowe, Amy Kwok, Elizabeth Patitsas, Presley Perswain, Jeremiah Tantongco, Christopher Head</p>
<!--break--><p>Mission Statement</p>
<ul>
<li>David: should reflect our desire for encouraging volunteer opportunities (should we include the broader community, beyond the CS dept?)</li>
<li>Michele: CS dept encourages student participation in department decision making and committee involvement; discuss adding this?</li>
<li>David: dept would like us to encourage students to be involved in dept decision-making</li>
<li>Elizabeth: most students don&apos;t know committee opportunities exist</li>
<li>Amy: the dayplanners now have a list of opportunities including committee opportunities, but we still need more ways to advertise</li>
<li>Michele: dept plans to include a section on volunteer ops in the ugrad orientation</li>
<li>Elizabeth: use the all-students list to e-mail volunteer ops to students (Giuliana can arrange that)</li>
<li>Conclusion: draft statement is good but could add a small item on outside-department activities and volunteer ops</li>
<li>W.r.t. Networking, Jeremiah has an idea for an online networking opportunity</li>
</ul>
<p>Budget</p>
<ul>
<li>David: the department may not demand paid membership; if we think attracting members may be difficult then we might be able to go with unpaid membership</li>
<li>Michele: might be able to have individual membership but the department make a financial contribution on a non-membership basis</li>
<li>Presley: AMS minimum is $1</li>
<li>Jeremiah: what do we offer members that we don&apos;t offer nonmembers? Subsidized food sales? (would require membership checking and printed cards)</li>
<li>Presley: problem: the AMS requires waivers to be signed for each membership; this was ignored in earlier years but may no longer be possible especially due to the constitution rewrite</li>
<li>Elizabeth: do we need to be in the AMS? Some clubs aren&apos;t (math club)</li>
<li>Presley: would be really difficult to separate from the AMS</li>
<li>Chris: there was a suggestion of including $1 of something for free with every membership</li>
<li>Jeremiah: membership fees aren&apos;t about the money, they&apos;re about the AMS legality; can we leave everything alone for this year and make a plan for attracting members next year?</li>
</ul>
<p>Lockers</p>
<ul>
<li>David: a good potential source of revenue; valuable service to students; could create a social zone near the lockers; could also advertise the CSSS... question: how much are people willing to pay? Department is willing to discuss plenty of different options for financing; CSSS should prepare a proposal. First activity, circulate a survey asking students if they&apos;re interested in lockers and how much they&apos;d pay? $10 per month was discussed; it was thought students might not be interested at that price.</li>
<li>Presley: lockers go anywhere up to $60 per year on the rest of campus</li>
<li>Nadia: Kaiser charges just $10 for a full year! But not very useful for CS students as building is locked after 6pm.</li>
<li>Michele: need to decide by December else we&apos;d have to reapply for approval from fire marshal</li>
<li>Jeremiah: can we do a pilot project with a smaller number of lockers and install the rest later?</li>
<li>David: we may be able to get the second half approved for installation later than December if we already have the first half</li>
<li>David: we might be able to mitigate the financial risk by arranging a loan payment based on locker income so CSSS doesn&apos;t lose money due to low demand</li>
</ul>
<p>Second Lounge</p>
<ul>
<li>It&apos;s getting crowded in the office; things have been stolen occasionally; we&apos;ve had noise complaints from the learning centre</li>
<li>Llachlan has asked (or will ask, at its next meeting) the spacecomm</li>
<li>David: sounds like a good idea; one issue is noise w.r.t. adjacent labs (but that&apos;s no worse than our current location)</li>
<li>Elizabeth: advantageous because it&apos;s beside the undergrad labs</li>
<li>Presley: we&apos;d like to lock 021 and then move TV/games/etc into 021 and keep 021 open</li>
<li>David: how much use does 021 get now?</li>
<li>Elizabeth: very little use currently: occasional sleeping, occasional microwave use</li>
<li>David: doesn&apos;t see any major problems but it will probably be a big discussion in spacecomm</li>
<li>Nadia: re: noise: west wing has better walls than X and will attenuate noise better</li>
</ul>
<p>Events</p>
<ul>
<li>Michele: do we have any planned portfolio of events yet?</li>
<li>Jeremiah: was contacted about setting up talks with industry professionals</li>
<li>Michele: we already have tech talks and such from many companies; risk: we&apos;d prefer not to invite too many people who are just interested in recruiting</li>
<li>David: should we move some existing talks to be co-sponsored by CSSS? We could advertise etc.</li>
<li>Michele: do students want more talks? One time the dept had someone ask to present a talk who was interested in marketing a product; need to try to avoid things like that. Michele could contact companies when requested by students instead of other way around?</li>
<li>Jeremiah: had the idea of a less technical, less career-oriented, and more casual event</li>
<li>David: does CSSS want to co-sponsor the CS town hall (the majors/honours/etc lunches)? We could send some executives to the events and talk about ourselves, hear from students? It&apos;s a chance for students to ask questions and make comments.</li>
</ul>
<p>Meeting adjourned at 7:05pm.</p>
